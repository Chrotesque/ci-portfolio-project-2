Help

Simon Says is a simple game of memorizing the order of buttons pressed by the game and then repeating the order yourself.

The game consists of steps, sequences and rounds.

A step is each time either you or the game presses buttons.

The order in which the buttons are pressed is the sequence which is randomly generated as the game goes on, however previously pressed buttons remain the same until the end of the round.

Once the entire sequence has been either replicated correctly or incorrectly by the player, the round ends and a new one starts.

There are 3 difficulty levels:

  • Easy - The sequences are played slow, you have to deal with 2 buttons and can repeat a step upon making a mistake.
  • Normal - The sequences are played a bit faster, you have to deal with 4 buttons and a single mistake will end the game.
  • Custom - This is where we leave the phrase "simple game" behind and the settings menu comes into full play, allowing for incremental changes to suit your needs and wants.
